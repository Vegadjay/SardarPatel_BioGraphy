Vallabhbhai Patel (October 31, 1875 - December 15, 1950), the icon of India's solidarity and integrity and the champion of peasant's movement in British India, was a political and social leader of India who played a major role in the country's struggle for Independence and subsequently guided its integration into a united, Independent nation. He is known as the 'Iron Man of India'. After the great victory of Bardoli peasants' movement, people named him ‘Sardar'. Vallabhbhai Patel already had a successful practice as a lawyer when he was first inspired by the work and philosophy of Mahatma Gandhi. Vallabhbhai Patel subsequently organised the peasants of Kheda, Borsad, and Bardoli in Gujarat in a non-violent Civil Disobedience Movement against oppressive policies imposed by the British Raj. In this role, he became the most influential leader in Gujarat under guidance of Mahatma Gandhi. He rose to the leadership of the Indian National Congress in 1931 at the Karachi Congress Session and was at the forefront of all political events from 1931 onwards, at national level. He was a key figure in organising the party for elections in 1934 and 1937 and promoting the Quit India Movement of 1942, which resulted into arrest of almost all the leaders of freedom struggle.

As the first Home Minister and Deputy Prime Minister of India, Sardar Patel organised relief for refugees in Punjab and Delhi as well as led efforts to restore peace across the nation. Sardar Patel took charge of the task to forge a united India from the 562 semi-autonomous princely states and British-era colonial provinces. Using frank diplomacy backed with the option (and the use) of military action, Sardar Patel's leadership enabled the accession of almost every princely state. Hailed as the Iron Man of India, he is also remembered as the 'Patron Saint' of India's civil servants for establishing modern all-India services. Sardar Patel was also one of the earliest proponents of property rights and free enterprise in India. His contribution as the Chairman of the Constitutional Committee on Fundamental Rights, Minorities' rights, Provincial Constitutions, and Demarcation of Borders of Scheduled Areas are the pillars of the Indian Constitution.

Historical Walk

After India gained independence from British rule in 1947, the country was divided into two dominions: India and Pakistan. This partition resulted in the creation of numerous princely states, which were given the option to join either India or Pakistan. Patel, as the first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister of independent India, was tasked with the monumental challenge of integrating these princely states into the Indian Union. Patel's approach was characterized by persuasive diplomacy, negotiations, and, when necessary, firmness. His historical walk across the newly independent nation involved visiting various princely states to convince their rulers to accede to India. His efforts were crucial in ensuring the unity and territorial integrity of India. By the time of his death in 1950, almost all princely states had acceded to India, with only a few exceptions like Kashmir.

Historical Oath Taking Ceremony

The oath-taking ceremony held on December 15, 1950, was a grand and solemn affair, attended by dignitaries, political leaders, and members of the public. It symbolized the formal assumption of responsibility by Patel for the governance and security of the nation. The oath-taking ceremony was conducted in accordance with constitutional procedures and protocols, underscoring the democratic foundations of the Indian state. During his tenure as Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister, Patel focused on consolidating the newly independent nation and addressing the myriad challenges it faced. His efforts were instrumental in laying the groundwork for India's administrative and institutional framework, as well as shaping its foreign policy and defense strategies.

A Formal Gathering Under the Open Sky

One notable example of a formal gathering under the open sky featuring Sardar Patel could be his participation in the "Unity Campaign" during the integration of princely states into the Indian Union. Patel traveled extensively across the country, addressing public gatherings and meetings to garner support for the integration process. These gatherings played a crucial role in mobilizing public opinion and building consensus for Patel's vision of a unified India. Given Patel's reputation as a charismatic orator and a skilled communicator, these gatherings would have been significant occasions, drawing crowds from diverse backgrounds and regions. Patel's speeches were known for their clarity, conviction, and ability to inspire action among the audience.

A Moment of Reverence

One such moment of reverence occurred after his passing on December 15, 1950. The nation mourned the loss of this iconic leader, and numerous events were held across the country to pay tribute to his contributions. Mass gatherings, prayer meetings, and memorial services were organized to honor Patel's memory and legacy. His funeral procession in Mumbai was attended by millions, reflecting the profound respect and reverence people held for him. Another moment of reverence associated with Sardar Patel is the annual observance of his birth anniversary on October 31st. This day, known as Rashtriya Ekta Diwas (National Unity Day), is dedicated to commemorating Patel's role in integrating princely states into the Indian Union and promoting national unity. On this occasion, various events, seminars, and programs are held across India to celebrate Patel's life, values, and contributions to the nation.
